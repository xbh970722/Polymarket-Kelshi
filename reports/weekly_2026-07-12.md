## 3 lessons this week
1. **The freeze is doing exactly what it was designed to do.** Daily risk deployment fell $176→$1.80 and
   Brier(model) flipped from losing to beating Brier(market) within 3 days of FREEZE-14 landing (07-09→07-11).
   Correlation isn't proof the freeze *caused* the calibration flip, but the timing lines up with the channel
   shutdowns (shortcycle/favorites off) that came with it — less noise, cleaner signal.
2. **Two very different P&L numbers can both be true and both matter.** Settled-calibration P&L (+$5.92) says
   the model is now beating the market on resolved bets. Full ledger net (−$17.27) says the week still lost
   money, dominated by −$55.56 of pre-freeze swing-exit churn that has nothing to do with model accuracy.
   Reporting only the flattering number would have been a lie by omission — both belong in every future weekly.
3. **Large model/market divergence is a two-edged signal, and this week had a clean example of each edge.**
   KXHIGHNY-T81 (50pt divergence, model right, +$2.65) rewarded trusting a concrete forecast model over a
   confident market. KXLLM1 (35pt divergence, blind estimators) was voided by verification because the
   *market* turned out to be reading a better information source. Divergence alone doesn't tell you which
   side is behind — only checking the market's information set does.
